<h2 style="text-align: left;">Vashon Island CERT:<a href="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_2.png"><img title="image" style="border:0px none;   background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; float: right;" alt="image" src="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_thumb.png" width="270" height="162" align="right" /></a></h2>
<ul>
    <li>Sponsored by Vashon Island Fire &amp; Rescue&hellip;.CERT is both a basic <span style="text-decoration: underline;">course</span> and an active local <span style="text-decoration: underline;">team</span> of citizens prepared to support the Fire Dept. or Sheriff</li>
    <li>A partner with VashonBePrepared, the local network of citizen groups and government agencies dedicated to leading our island in preparedness and response</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="" src="/portals/1/Graphics/Logos/VIFR.gif" /></p>
<p><img style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto;" alt="VashonBePrepared.org - Vashon's Disaster Preparedness Coalition" src="/Portals/1/Graphics/Logos/VashonBePrepared.433x80trans.gif?ver=2015-03-15-123631-657" /></p>
<p>Participation in CERT is highly rewarding. If community support when problems arise is important to you, CERT will likely be an extraordinarily positive experience.</p>
<p>CERT originated in Los Angeles in 1994 to support the city fire department, and has been enhanced and extended to hundreds of communities by FEMA to train citizens to safely volunteer in various emergencies.</p>
<h2>Rewards of being on the active team:</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Increased safety at your home &amp; business</li>
    <li>Increased personal preparedness for windstorms, earthquakes and power outages</li>
    <li>New life-saving and first aid skills</li>
    <li>Increased self-confidence</li>
    <li>New friends</li>
    <li>Participation in the Vashon community Stylish green hard-hat and hi-vis vest (for team)</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_8.png"><img title="image" style="border:0px none;   background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px;" alt="image" src="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_thumb_3.png" width="622" height="462" /></a></p>
<h2>Requirements to be on the team:</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Vashon resident &ndash; full-time or part-time</li>
    <li>Very basic physical fitness</li>
    <li>Basic CERT course (3 parts described on this flyer)</li>
    <li>Several additional Vashon-specific trainings (maps, two-way radios, search &amp; rescue practice, etc.)</li>
    <li>Four refresher or enrichment trainings each year</li>
    <li>Comply with CERT policies and codes of conduct</li>
    <li>Favorable background check</li>
    <li>Responsibility and accountability</li>
</ul>
<h2><a href="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_10.png"><img title="image" style="border:0px none;   background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px;" alt="image" src="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_thumb_4.png" width="648" height="487" /></a></h2>
<h2>Typical activations include</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Search for missing persons (Vashon has no other local search/rescue group)</li>
    <li>Logistical support to the Fire Dept. or Sheriff</li>
    <li>Information gathering during a disaster; First aid and information stations</li>
</ul>
<h2>CERT does not&hellip;</h2>
<p> fight fires, go into unstable buildings, or neglect our own neighborhoods. CERT members are specially trained to be a skilled resource in their neighborhoods, and respond to the greater community only after their own home and neighborhood are stable.</p>
<p><a href="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_12.png"><img title="image" style="border:0px none;   background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px;" alt="image" src="./images/409/Windows-Live-Writer-866302389058_883C-image_thumb_5.png" width="627" height="471" /></a></p>
<h2>2018 Course Dates</h2>
<p>The program is offered at no cost to the public. Options include:</p>
<p><strong>March 31:</strong> 5 hours of household preparedness for disasters and power outages, plus fire &amp; gas safety</p>
<p><strong>Friday evenings, April 6 thru May 11:</strong> 24 more hours of emergency response training for the average citizen: first aid, light search and rescue, light fire-fighting and fire/chemical safety</p>
<p><strong>Friday evenings May 18, 25 and June 8 plus Sunday June 3:</strong> 45 MORE hours of training in organizing ourselves into an effective team, plus practice in simulated disaster scenarios.</p>
<h2>The 45 hour basic course covers:</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Safety training around utility problems such as gas leaks and damaged power lines</li>
    <li>Reducing potential fire and electrical hazards in your home and business</li>
    <li>Extinguishing various types of small fires</li>
    <li>First aid including head-to-toe assessments, wound care, splinting, triage of multiple patients, and more</li>
    <li>Selecting and setting up a medical treatment area for a disaster situation</li>
    <li>How to size-up various emergency situations and respond safely and effectively</li>
    <li>Searching structures and the Vashon community or woods for lost persons</li>
    <li>How Vashon&rsquo;s CERT team operates, our responsibilities and coordinating with other organizations</li>
</ul>
<h2>Supplemental topics and classes after joining the team:</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Use of two-way radios (both Ham radios and ordinary &ldquo;walkie-talkies&rdquo;)</li>
    <li>Reading various maps, finding addresses, using a compass and your phone to navigate</li>
    <li>The nitty-gritty of supporting Vashon Fire Dept. and the King County Sheriff</li>
    <li>Several types of search operations and how to safely conduct each one</li>
    <li>Advanced first aid</li>
    <li>And more&hellip;</li>
    <li>the list is long and the topics are interesting!</li>
</ul>
